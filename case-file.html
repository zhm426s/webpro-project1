The Murder of Eleanor Whitmore

The Basics:
Eleanor Whitmore was murdered around 9:17 PM on March 14, 2023 inside her private office at Whitmore Gallery during the gallery's anniversary event. Her body was discovered at approximately 9:26 PM. The cause of death was determined to be blunt force trauma to the back of the head.

Security cameras were disabled between 9:05 PM and 9:58 PM, leaving a critical window where the crime occurred without recorded footage. A shattered wine glass was found near her body and the office door was locked from the inside.

Shortly before her death, Eleanor sent a message reading:
“Meet me in the office. We need to settle this tonight.”

About the Victim:
Eleanor Whitmore was the founder and sole owner of Whitmore Gallery. She had built a reputation for ambitious exhibitions and aggressive business expansion. She recently announced plans to open a second gallery location later that year.

Despite the gallery's public success, internal financial records suggest she was under significant financial stress. Her relationships with investors, artists, and employees had recently become strained.

There are four potential suspects connected to Eleanor who all had possible motives and access during the event.
